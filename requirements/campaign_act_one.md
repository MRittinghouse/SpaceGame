## Act One

### INTRODUCTION: 

You, the protagonist (player can choose their own name), are the son of an assistant foreman for an asteroid mining operation in the orbit of Nexus Prime. You grew up in an impoverished community, bled dry by corporate greed and wage slavery. As a child, you watched your father pull 16 hour shifts consistently, working himself into an early grave. You took some solace in the fact that your father had worked himself up to some modicum of seniority; though it was a meaningless title with no authority. 

Between the stress and the poor air conditioning systems in your colony ship, your father passed away six months ago, at the age of 43. You had only just celebrated your 16th birthday. You had some schooling, but your general education remains incomplete. You have no path forward in life. You cannot be accepted into any of the universities, are unqualified for any relevant positions in the system. You have no other family to take care of you. 

You consider staying aboard the colony ship, taking on some menial position. Perhaps in maintenance, or as a laborer for the mining operation. But without anyone to take you in, you realize you'd merely be sinking into the same wage slavery that killed your father. You'd take on loans to rent unlivable living quarters. You'd rack up debt while earning meager credits. At night, as you toss and turn weighing your options, it torments you to know that you'd be resigning yourself to the same cycle as your father. Nothing gets better. Nothing will improve. 

Perhaps there is another path. With the last of your father's savings, you spend a few days cleaning up his last few possessions of value, before pawning them off at the ship's trading post. You can barely afford the hull of a broken shuttle. You convince one of your neighbors in maintenance to assist you with sourcing an engine, which also was in dire need of repair. As your credits dry out, you only just barely manage to get the shuttle working, and charge the fuel rods. 

Departing from the colony ship, you find yourself in the orbit of Nexus Prime, with the system at your disposal. You have 2,000 credits to your name. You are woefully unaware of the system, its politics, its people. Your goal is simple - to survive the system and find new horizons - anywhere away from here. 

=======

### Mission 01: 

As you land on Nexus Prime, you are greeted by an overwhelming view. Towering skyscrapers, neon lights of all shapes and colors. The port is bustling. Hundreds of ships line the horizon. Uniformed men inspect the cargo - though your hold is entirely empty minus your pitiful living supplies. You are temporarily dazed by the shift in scenery. 

"I need your bill of landing, and your registration, sir." Customs Officer Larsen interrupts your loitering. 

"Here is my registration, but I'm afraid I don't have a bill of landing." 

"Then I'm afraid we'll be confiscating any goods in your hull, as you are not cleared to import goods at this time." 

"The only items in the hull are personal living supplies."

"You'll be able to reclaim those once you pay the 250 credit fee to acquire your bill of landing."

At this point, the player will be forced to pay the 250 credits to acquire a bill of landing, which permits trade in the system for this faction. A bill of landing is required for each faction. 

"Please wait in your shuttle for an hour while we process the paperwork for you." Officer Larssen says. 
A short time passes. 

"With your bill of landing in hand, you'll now be able to trade freely and import or export goods from our port." 

=====

### Mission 02: 

You make your way to the trade center. A hustling bazaar, with a dizzying array of people in different uniforms and outfits, carrying carts. Garages load cargo in and out. Displays light up with the latest prices, and the day's news. The room smells of oil. 

A hooded man with four missing teeth looks at you intently. "Buy it for less than you sell it, kid." 

He spits, and then stumbles away. 

You continue to make your way down the pathway, until you are stopped by another man, this time in a bright blue outfit. 

"You there. I'll give you 500 credits to ship this iron ore off to Forgeworks. I just bought it, and I've been told that my carrier is full up. I need it there within the week." 

MISSION - If you accept it, you are given 10 iron ore to deliver to Forgeworks. Once you land on Forgeworks, another person in a matching bright blue uniform will take it from you and reward you with 500 credits.
