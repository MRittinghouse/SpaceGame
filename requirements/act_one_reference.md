# Act One: Narrative Reference

> Consolidated reference for Act One narrative systems. For detailed original design docs, see requirements/archive/

---

## Story Overview

**Title**: "New Horizons"
**Setting**: The Aurelia Expanse, 2335 CE, a remote 10-system cluster at the edge of colonized space.

The player is a 16-year-old orphan whose father, an assistant mining foreman, died from failing air recyclers on a Nexus Prime orbital. With a broken shuttle and 2,000 credits, the player arrives at Nexus Prime knowing nobody. Over the course of 17 missions across five chapters, they build a crew, develop faction relationships, and uncover a conspiracy called **The Ledger**: a rogue faction within the Commerce Guild working to monopolize the Expanse through manufactured pirate attacks.

**Emotional arc**: Isolation, Curiosity, Belonging, Unease, Resolve

**Central theme**: "You can't survive alone." The player's journey from isolated orphan to connected captain mirrors the political reality that the factions need cooperation to face the pirate threat but cannot agree on terms.

**Opening state**: Nobody. Alone, broke, unskilled, grieving.
**Closing state**: Somebody. A known trader with crew, faction standing, evidence of a conspiracy, and a major choice about who to trust.

### The Ledger

The central antagonist is not revealed until Missions 16-17. The Ledger is a shadow organization within the Commerce Guild's upper ranks. Their goal: destabilize independent shipping through coordinated pirate attacks, drive up security costs, then position the Guild as the only solution. They seek total commercial control of the Expanse. The name is a dark joke on the Guild's sacred contracts and account books.

The Act One ending neutralizes one pirate base but the leadership escapes. Recovered data reveals operations in uncharted space beyond the Expanse's borders, setting up Act Two.

## Chapter and Mission Structure

### Chapter 1: Arrival (Missions 01-03)

**Theme**: The player is small, the world is big. Learn to survive.
**Systems introduced**: Trading, cargo delivery, interstellar travel.

| # | Title | Summary |
|---|-------|---------|
| 01 | Bill of Landing | Customs Officer Larsen demands 250 credits for a trade permit. Establishes bureaucracy and the permit system. |
| 02 | Iron Ore Delivery | Deliver 10 iron ore to Forgeworks for 500 credits. First cargo job. |
| 03 | Footing the Bill | Elena Reeves offers trading advice at the cantina. Introduction to the trade loop and first crew opportunity. |

### Chapter 2: Making Connections (Missions 04-07)

**Theme**: Meet the factions. Understand the sector's politics and people.
**Systems introduced**: Faction culture, mining, passenger missions, tariff mechanics.

| # | Title | Summary |
|---|-------|---------|
| 04 | Union Territory | Travel to Breakstone. Hanna Voss demands proof of good faith: deliver food to the commissary. Earn a Union trade permit. |
| 05 | The Foreman's Son | Marcus Jin recognizes the player's surname. He filed a buried report about the air recyclers. Mining tutorial follows. |
| 06 | The Scholar's Errand | Transport Dr. Priya Osei to Axiom Labs. She studies resource extraction economics and frames human suffering as data. |
| 07 | The Drifter's Deal | Tomas Drifter at Haven's Rest proposes routing textiles past Guild tariffs. First moral gray area. Player can accept or decline. |

### Chapter 3: Undercurrents (Missions 08-11)

**Theme**: Something is wrong. Pirate attacks are coordinated. Factions blame each other.
**Systems introduced**: Encounters, salvage, espionage, political observation.

| # | Title | Summary |
|---|-------|---------|
| 08 | Cargo Lost | Distress signal from a raided freighter. Player chooses to help or press on. Meet Captain Reva Sato, who reveals coordinated attack patterns. |
| 09 | Whispers at the Bar | Faction argument overheard at the cantina. Dex Halloran offers pirate intel in exchange for delivering a data chip to Crimson Reach. |
| 10 | The Crimson Run | Deliver Dex's chip to Malia Torres at Crimson Reach. She reveals the pirates have Guild-grade military hardware. |
| 11 | Embassy Visit | Faction summit at Axiom Labs ends in deadlock. Player may spot a suspicious signal exchange between a Guild delegate and an unknown figure. |

### Chapter 4: Escalation (Missions 12-15)

**Theme**: The stakes become personal. Investigation leads to proof and a defining choice.
**Systems introduced**: Combat, speech/persuasion, resource gathering, major branching choice.

| # | Title | Summary |
|---|-------|---------|
| 12 | Under Fire | First pirate attack on the player. Military-grade weapons, coordinated tactics. Marcus (if present) identifies Guild hardware. |
| 13 | The Favor Returned | Meet Oren Tak in Breakstone's mining tunnels. He reveals unmarked ships docking at a hidden facility near Iron Depths. |
| 14 | Deep Scan | Priya modifies the scanner. Player gathers materials, then scans the Iron Depths asteroid belt to confirm the pirate command base. |
| 15 | The Briefing | Present evidence to faction leaders. Choose one faction's approach for the assault: Guild (military strike), Union (blockade), Collective (data infiltration), or Alliance (small-team stealth). |

### Chapter 5: The Revelation (Missions 16-17)

**Theme**: Climax and aftermath. Execute the plan. Discover The Ledger.
**Systems introduced**: Path-specific gameplay (combat, negotiation, stealth, exploration).

| # | Title | Summary |
|---|-------|---------|
| 16 | The Operation | Execute the chosen faction's plan against the pirate base. All paths reveal The Ledger conspiracy. Base neutralized, leaders escape. |
| 17 | New Horizons | Aftermath and reflection. Crew reflections, faction reactions, new coordinates in uncharted space. Act One concludes. |

## Key NPCs and Their Roles

### Companions (Optional Crew)

All recruitment is optional. Solo play is always viable. Each companion requires the player to earn their trust through action.

**Elena Reeves** (Navigator, Commerce Guild background)
- Met at: Nexus Prime, Mission 03
- Recruitment effort: Low (single conversation)
- Personality: Sharp, guarded, precise. Guild speech patterns she is trying to unlearn. Left the Guild after discovering route pilot underpayment. She needs a ship, not a friend.
- Narrative role: Provides Guild insider perspective. Comments on pirate intel (M09), offers navigation expertise throughout.

**Marcus Jin** (Engineer, Miners Union)
- Met at: Breakstone, Mission 05
- Recruitment effort: Medium (fetch quest + return trip)
- Personality: Gruff, professional, principled. Carries guilt about the buried air recycler report. Hums mining shanties. Shares food instinctively.
- Narrative role: Personal connection to the player's backstory. Identifies Guild hardware on pirate ships (M12). Vouches for the player with Union contacts (M13).

**Dr. Priya Osei** (Scientist, Science Collective)
- Met at: Nexus Prime then Axiom Labs, Mission 06
- Recruitment effort: High (transport + data gathering + return)
- Personality: Brilliant, clinically detached, genuinely kind but socially awkward. Uses full titles. Describes everything as data.
- Narrative role: Modifies scanner for the base detection (M14). Provides analytical perspective on the conspiracy.

**Tomas Drifter** (Trader, Frontier Alliance)
- Met at: Haven's Rest, Mission 07
- Recruitment effort: Low-medium (gray trade + delivery run)
- Personality: Charming, morally flexible, speaks in proverbs. Feeds strangers instinctively. Running from something.
- Narrative role: Introduces moral gray areas. Offers the pragmatic small-team approach (M15-16).

## Dialogue Flag System

### Naming Conventions

- All flags use `snake_case`
- Boolean only: flags are True or absent (never integers or strings)
- Flags are permanent once set (never unset within a playthrough)
- Prefix patterns:
  - `met_<npc>`: first meeting with an NPC
  - `chose_<path>`: mutually exclusive player choice
  - `<npc>_recruited` / `<npc>_declined` / `<npc>_dismissed`: crew state
  - Verb-based: `helped_`, `delivered_`, `attended_`, `completed_`, `survived_`

### Implementation

- Flags stored in `player.dialogue_flags` (dict[str, bool])
- Mission prerequisites use `required_flags` field
- Dialogue conditions use `required_flags` / `excluded_flags` on responses
- Flag-setting rewards use `set_flag` reward type
- Forced encounters use `trigger_flag` for once-only gating

### Critical Path Flags

These flags are required for Act One progression:

```
M03: talked_to_elena_cantina
M04: breakstone_permit_earned, met_hanna_voss
M05: met_marcus_jin, completed_mining_tutorial
M06: met_priya_osei, escorted_priya_axiom
M07: met_tomas_drifter, (accepted or declined)_drifter_deal
M08: encountered_distress_signal, met_reva_sato, pirate_conspiracy_awareness
M09: met_dex_halloran, accepted_dex_favor
M10: delivered_dex_chip, met_malia_torres, malia_pirate_intel
M11: attended_summit, summit_deadlock, faction_positions_known
M12: survived_pirate_attack, combat_tutorial_complete
M13: met_oren_tak, oren_revealed_base
M14: scanned_pirate_base, pirate_base_confirmed, has_scan_data

Each companion follows a progression chain. Example for Marcus:

```

  -> marcus_fetch_accepted
    -> marcus_fetch_completed
      -> marcus_offered_position
        -> marcus_recruited (or marcus_declined)

## Journal System

### Purpose

The journal is a player-facing narrative log, separate from the Mission Log. The Mission Log tracks objectives. The journal tracks meaning. It has two components:

1. **Auto-entries**: Factual, ship's-log-style entries generated at key story beats (one per mission, 17 total in Act One). Read-only. Written in neutral tone to avoid putting words in the player's mouth.
2. **Player entries**: Freeform text (max 500 characters) with optional tags. Freely editable and deletable.

### Data Model

`JournalEntry` fields: entry_id, text, game_day, system_id, source ("auto"/"player"), tag, mission_id, created_at.

`Journal` operations:
- `add_auto_entry()`: idempotent (duplicate entry_id is a no-op)
- `add_player_entry()`: creates a new player entry with timestamp
- `edit_player_entry()` / `delete_player_entry()`: refuses to modify auto entries
- `get_entries()`: returns chronological order, filterable by tag or source

### Tags

Four player-facing tags: **People**, **Places**, **Suspicions**, **Goals**. When a tag filter is active, auto-entries are hidden. The "All" filter shows everything. Tags are the player's organizational tool.

### Auto-Entry Triggers

Auto-entries fire from mission completion, `set_flag` dialogue actions, and specific story events. Some entries have conditional text based on player choices or crew presence.

Example entries:

- **M05** (Marcus): "Met Marcus Jin at Breakstone. Mining foreman, twenty years on station. He filed an engineering report about the air recyclers on the Nexus Prime orbital. The report was buried. My father died six months later."
- **M07** (Tomas, accepted path): "Met Tomas Drifter at Haven's Rest. Accepted a gray-market trade: rerouting supplies past Guild tariffs to people who need them. Haven's Rest runs on handshakes and trust."
- **M16** (The Ledger): "The pirate operation is funded by a rogue faction within the Commerce Guild. They call themselves The Ledger. Goal: destabilize shipping, monopolize security, control the Expanse. Evidence recovered. Base neutralized. Leaders escaped."

### Auto-Entry JSON Format

Stored in `data/journal/entries.json`. Each entry has: `entry_id`, `mission_id`, `trigger_flag`, and `text`. Branching missions use separate entries with different `trigger_flag` values.

### Interface

- Accessed via galaxy map button or pause menu
- Quick-add overlay (press J) available from most views for writing notes without opening the full journal
- Visual distinction: auto-entries have a terminal icon, player entries have a pen icon with edit/delete controls
- Keyboard: Escape (close), N (new entry), Tab (cycle filters), Up/Down (scroll)

### Serialization

Journal serializes inside Player's save chain: `SaveManager -> Player.to_dict() -> Journal.to_dict()`. Missing `journal` key in old saves creates an empty Journal.

## Faction Path Branching (Mission 15-16)

The player's choice in Mission 15 determines Mission 16's gameplay:

| Faction | Leader | Approach | Gameplay |
|---------|--------|----------|----------|
| Commerce Guild | Reva Sato | Military strike | Space combat, escort flight |
| Miners Union | Hanna Voss | Workers' blockade | Negotiation, intimidation |
| Science Collective | Dr. Priya Osei | Covert infiltration | Data mining, hacking |
| Frontier Alliance | Tomas Drifter | Small-team stealth | Ground exploration, infiltration |

All paths lead to the same core revelation (The Ledger), but through different gameplay and with different faction relationship consequences.

## Narrative Quality Guidelines

These principles guide all Act One writing.

### Tone

Hopeful but gritty. The player starts with nothing and builds something real. The world is unfair but worth fighting for. People are complicated. Every faction has legitimate grievances and genuine flaws.

### Show, Don't Tell

- Mission briefings should describe what the player sees (empty supply racks, rationing medics), not explain why they should care.
- Companion competence should be demonstrated before it is stated. Elena is seen fixing something before she tells you she is good at it.
- Danger should be felt through hostile scans, crew unease, damaged infrastructure, and higher prices, not just a UI label.
- The Ledger conspiracy should emerge from patterns the player notices (cargo manifests that don't add up, officials who know things they shouldn't), not from NPCs saying "something bigger is going on."

### Faction Voice Consistency

Each faction's NPCs should sound distinct:

- **Commerce Guild**: Transactional, euphemistic, calculating. "High-risk opportunity" instead of "dangerous."
- **Miners Union**: Blunt, practical, slightly bitter. Short sentences. Skeptical of people with big vocabularies.
- **Science Collective**: Precise, curious, occasionally oblivious to social cues. Clinical detachment.
- **Frontier Alliance**: Idealistic but tired. Proverbs and stories. Handshake culture.
- **Crimson Reach independents**: Guarded, transactional, zero small talk. Information is currency.

### Companion Writing

Companions should push back on the player, not just agree:

- **Elena**: Challenges technical shortcuts. "That's a shortcut. Shortcuts get people killed in vacuum."
- **Marcus**: Questions moral compromises. "I've seen where that road goes."
- **Priya**: Calls out inefficiency. "We're burning fuel on sentiment."
- **Tomas**: Worries about unseen consequences. "The people we're leaving behind will remember."

### Dialogue Choices

Player dialogue options should reveal character, not restate the same sentiment:

- Pragmatic: "What's the pay?"
- Empathetic: "How long have they been waiting?"
- Suspicious: "Who else knows about this?"
- Reckless: "When do we leave?"

### Worldbuilding in Detail

- Each system should have a sensory signature (Nexus Prime: hum of transactions, holographic ads; Crimson Reach: silence between alarms, ozone smell; Breakstone: machine oil, communal cooking).
- Travel log entries should convey how a place feels, not just what it is.
- Station arrivals should vary by danger level. Safe hubs feel oppressively crowded. Dangerous systems feel abandoned.
- History should be visible in the environment: old colony markings, over-repaired equipment, monuments to events the player learns about later.

### Writing Style Rules

- Avoid GenAI writing tropes. See `requirements/archive/` for the full banned-pattern list.
- Do not use em-dashes. Use colons, commas, semicolons, or restructure the sentence.
- Do not use "no X, no Y" parallel constructions.
- NPCs who exist purely to teach mechanics feel artificial. Integrate tutorial information into natural encounters.
- Ambient dialogue should reference player actions, include overheard arguments and dark humor, and occasionally be wrong or misleading.
