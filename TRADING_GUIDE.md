# Trading Guide - How the Economy Works

## 💰 **How Prices Work**

### **Price Formula:**
```
Final Price = Base Price × (1 + Supply/Demand Modifier + Random Variance)
```

### **Three Factors Affect Prices:**

#### **1. Base Price** (fixed per commodity)
Each commodity has a base price set in the game data.

Examples:
- Food: 50 CR
- Fuel: 10 CR (now affordable!)
- Electronics: 200 CR
- Exotic Goods: 600 CR

#### **2. Supply & Demand** (±25% based on system type)
- **Systems that PRODUCE a good** → 25% CHEAPER
- **Systems that CONSUME a good** → 25% MORE EXPENSIVE

**Example: Food (base price 50 CR)**
- **Verdant** (agricultural, produces food): ~37 CR (25% discount)
- **Forgeworks** (industrial, needs food): ~62 CR (25% premium)
- **Profit margin**: 25 CR per unit (67% profit!)

#### **3. Random Daily Variance** (±5-10%)
Small fluctuations that change each game day (when you travel).
- Adds unpredictability
- Prevents exact price prediction
- Makes timing matter slightly

---

## 📅 **When Do Prices Change?**

**Prices update:** EVERY TIME YOU TRAVEL

- Travel from Nexus Prime → Verdant = **Day 2** = New prices
- Travel from Verdant → Forgeworks = **Day 3** = New prices again
- Stay at one system = Prices stay the same

**Important:** Prices are **per-system** and **per-day**:
- Verdant on Day 2 might have food at 38 CR
- Forgeworks on Day 2 might have food at 63 CR
- Verdant on Day 3 (after you return) = slightly different price (±5 CR)

## 🎯 **How to Trade Effectively**

### **Step 1: Understand Supply & Demand**

| System Type | Produces (CHEAP) | Consumes (EXPENSIVE) |
|-------------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Verdant** (Agricultural) | Food, Textiles | Machinery, Fuel |
| **Forgeworks** (Industrial) | Machinery, Metals | Food, Fuel |
| **Breakstone** (Mining) | Metals, Rare Metals | Food, Machinery |
| **Axiom Labs** (Research) | Electronics, Medical | Rare Metals, Food |
| **Nexus Prime** (Trade Hub) | Manufactured Goods | Everything (middling prices) |

### **Step 2: Find Profitable Routes**

**Beginner Route: Verdant ↔ Forgeworks**
1. Start at **Nexus Prime** (2,000 CR)
2. Travel to **Verdant**
3. Buy **Food** (cheap ~37 CR)
4. Travel to **Forgeworks**
5. Sell **Food** (expensive ~62 CR)
6. Buy **Machinery** (cheap here)
7. Return to Verdant and sell Machinery (expensive there)

**Profit per trip:** ~500-1,000 CR with starting ship!

### **Step 3: Watch the Trend Indicators**

The market shows price trends:
- **↓↓** = Very Low (GREAT for buying!)
- **↓** = Low (Good for buying)
- **→** = Normal
- **↑** = High (Good for selling)
- **↑↑** = Very High (GREAT for selling!)

### **New Fuel System:**
- **Fuel price:** Now only ~10 CR per unit (was 80 CR - you're welcome!)
- **Partial refueling:** Enter amount in quantity box, then click REFUEL
- **Full tank:** Leave quantity blank or enter 999

**Fuel Costs by Ship:**
- **Shuttle**: 10 fuel/jump × 10 CR = ~100 CR per jump
- Depends on distance (further = more fuel)

**Example Travel Costs:**
- Nexus Prime → Verdant (112 units): ~112 fuel = **~1,120 CR**
- Now affordable with 2,000 CR start!

## 💡 **Pro Tips**

1. **Buy in bulk when prices are low** (↓↓)
   - Watch your cargo space!

2. **Sell when prices spike** (↑↑)
   - Don't hold too long - prices change daily

3. **Specialize in triangular routes**
   - Buy where it's produced
   - Sell where it's consumed
   - Always carrying goods both ways

4. **Track your routes**
   - Verdant → Forgeworks → Breakstone → Nexus Prime
   - Each leg should make profit

5. **Manage fuel carefully**
   - Always keep 2-3 jumps worth of fuel
   - Refuel when fuel prices are low
   - Forgeworks produces fuel = cheap there!

6. **Start small, scale up**
   - Early game: Food trading (reliable, safe)
   - Mid game: Machinery & Electronics (better margins)
   - Late game: Rare Metals & Luxury goods (high risk/reward)
